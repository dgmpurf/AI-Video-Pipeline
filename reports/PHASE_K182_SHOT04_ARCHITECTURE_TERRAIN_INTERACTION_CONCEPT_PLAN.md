## 6. Why SHOT-04 Should Be New-Design, Not Patch-Design

SHOT-03 R01 preserved some corridor composition and column-edge staging value, but failed Shuangji identity. SHOT-03 R02 partially repaired Shuangji identity, but failed blocking, action relationship, and close-contact pressure.

The CUT_B/C recut route solved continuity better than another image2image insert loop. K180 Candidate B keeps SHOT-03 moving as a coherent corridor-combat piece.

SHOT-04 should therefore start with architecture interaction as its central premise. It should not be a mid-shot patch into SHOT-03, and it should not depend on R01/R02 as approved keyframes. The core lesson is simple but important: identity, blocking, and terrain contact must be designed from the beginning rather than retrofitted after the combat rhythm is already working.

## 7. SHOT-04 Design Principles

- Use one main architecture / terrain interaction only.
- Use one main space zone only.
- If later generated by CLI, target a 4-5 second source clip.
- If combat continues, require early contact within `0.15s-0.30s`.
- Use clear Fenshou / Shuangji actor causality: named attacker, named defender, contact point, reaction, and re-entry.
- Environmental damage may appear only after physical contact.
- No random breakage.
- No architecture damage without a specific contact point.
- No ordinary R03 image2image loop.
- If SHOT-04 remains combat, the final frame should cut mid-exchange, not settle into a poster pose.
- Identity and blocking must be treated as separate constraints:
  - identity references protect who Fenshou and Shuangji are;
  - composition / action references protect where bodies are and how force travels;
  - scene references protect world continuity only.

## 8. Candidate SHOT-04 Concepts

### A. Railing / Wooden Lattice Impact and Rebound

Premise: Fenshou shoulder-checks Shuangji's guard into a corridor railing or wooden lattice. Only the contact point cracks or flexes. Shuangji rebounds and counters.

Strength: Strong causal architecture interaction. This fits V1.11 damage-causality rules because the body/guard contact creates a specific environment response.

Risk: The prompt must avoid random breakage, overdestruction, and background structures cracking before contact.

### B. Doorway / Side-Hall Threshold Compression

Premise: Fenshou drives Shuangji toward a side-hall doorway. The doorframe briefly occludes part of the bodies. Shuangji pivots off the threshold and counters.

Strength: Strong spatial staging, natural occlusion, and a clear architecture-as-tactic premise without requiring broad destruction.

Risk: Could become static blocking if contact beats are weak or if the doorway becomes a visual frame rather than a pressure point.

### C. Pillar Occlusion Reboot, Designed From Scratch

Premise: A fresh SHOT-04 pillar interaction with both characters placed correctly from the first frame, not reusing failed SPLIT-02 R01/R02.

Strength: Could recover the original column idea with a clean design instead of a repair loop.

Risk: Highest risk because SHOT-03 SPLIT-02 already failed the identity/blocking trade-off twice.

### D. Rain Curtain / Smoke / Dust Concealment Re-Entry

Premise: A body or architecture impact throws water, dust, or smoke between the fighters. One fighter disappears behind the environmental veil and re-enters.

Strength: Cinematic and less dependent on precise structural breakage.

Risk: Concealment can hide faces, collapse identity clarity, or turn action into vague silhouettes if the re-entry is not explicit.

### E. Ground / Wet Stone Skid / Low Railing Pressure

Premise: Fenshou's pressure forces Shuangji into a wet-stone skid near a low railing. She catches the railing with forearm or shoulder and uses the rebound for a counter.

Strength: Terrain interaction without heavy destruction. Good for preserving identity and avoiding excessive architecture damage.

Risk: Could become footwork-only movement without a clear combat result unless the skid leads to a hard guard compression or counter contact.

## 10. Recommended Primary Route

Primary route for K183: **A. Railing / wooden lattice impact and rebound.**

Reason: It gives SHOT-04 the clearest new-design premise: architecture becomes part of the fight through direct body/guard contact, not as decorative background. It also maps cleanly to V1.11 damage-causality rules: contact point first, damage only after contact, fragments/cracks localized to the force direction, and immediate combat consequence.

Backup route for K183: **B. Doorway / side-hall threshold compression.**

Reason: It offers strong spatial staging and a natural occlusion device while keeping structural damage optional or minimal.

Avoid for now: **C. Pillar occlusion reboot**, unless manual pose/keyframe control is added first.
