## Remaining Limitation

V004 is still too close to ordinary corridor combat. The scene has columns, railings, wet stone floor, and temple architecture, but the action does not yet strongly use those elements as tactical fighting tools.

The intended escalation is terrain affordance: fighters using columns, wall surfaces, railing supports, wet floor, side structures, or architecture to change attack angle, compress guards, create displacement, and shape the fight.

The user examples include:

- Stepping on a wall, column, column base, or railing support.
- Using a column or wall to redirect attack angle.
- Parkour-like angle change without turning into floating or roof combat.
- Downward leg, fist, elbow, or knee attack from a terrain-assisted position.
- Body impact into a wooden door, lattice, wall panel, or side building.
- Smoke or dust concealment for suspense.
- Environmental objects such as stones, wood, pillars, or broken railing pieces used as thrown or countered objects.

These are examples, not a requirement to pack everything into one 5-second generation.

## V005 Recommendation

V005 should test one clear terrain-assisted attack in one stable exterior corridor area.

Recommended V005 direction:

- Keep the exterior corridor / columns / railing / wet stone floor as the single active zone.
- Do not start with large building destruction.
- Use one explicit terrain affordance only.
- Fenshou or Shuangji plants one foot on a column base, wall, or railing support.
- That terrain contact changes the attack angle.
- The attack lands as a downward elbow, fist, knee, or kick into the opponent's guard.
- The opponent reacts with hard guard compression, body displacement, short foot skid, and immediate recovery or re-entry.

V005 restrictions:

- No full wall-run.
