The architecture reference does not show action. A future package needs either text-planned composition or an additional approved action reference.

Action-composition needs:

- attacker motion direction
- defender/body contact direction

- whether impact is body-to-wall, guard-to-wall, shoulder-to-wall, or forearm/weaponless strike into guard
- readable body displacement into the wall
- wall-plane contact center and character spacing
- camera framing that preserves the side-wall geometry

Any action-composition reference should be duty-limited to motion layout and should not replace character identity or environment target duties.

Optional duties:

Not allowed:

- uncontrolled wall collapse
- entire building destruction
- large debris without contact cause
- fire/explosion/magic replacement of the physical impact

## 4. Impact Geometry

Future wall-impact planning should define the event in spatial terms before any package exists.

Required geometry decisions:

- wall plane: vertical dark wet wooden wall-panel from `A-SC-TEMPLE-SIDE-WALL-PANEL-002`
- impact center: a readable mid-height wall/panel area, not hidden by pillars
- contact direction: character momentum or guard compression must clearly drive toward the wall plane
- body or guard contact: choose one primary contact type, not several competing impact ideas
- camera orientation: side-corridor framing should keep the wall target readable and avoid jumping back to a generic courtyard
- corridor framing: pillars/eaves/wet floor remain supporting context, not the main impact object
- rain/wet-floor continuity: wet stone floor and rain atmosphere continue from the reference
- character-to-wall spatial relationship: one fighter must be visibly close enough to cause wall contact
- cause-effect readability: the wall effect must follow directly from body/guard contact, not appear spontaneously

Recommended initial impact concept:

One fighter is driven into the wooden wall-panel or guard-compressed against it; the wall target reacts locally at the impact center while the corridor architecture remains intact.

## 5. Controlled Breakage Scope

K204 recommends allowing minimal controlled breakage in the future package draft, but only if the next phase explicitly defines cause and impact center.

Allowed, if later authorized:

- localized cracks around a single impact center
- small wet wood splinters
- small rain spray and dust/mist at the contact point
- slight panel flex or deformation
- debris that travels outward from the exact impact point

Not allowed:

- uncontrolled wall collapse
- entire corridor destruction
- unexplained holes
- broken structure appearing before contact
- debris unrelated to impact direction
- large magical shockwave replacing body impact
- style drift away from rainy temple side-wall environment

Breakage rule:

No crack, splinter, hole, panel fracture, or debris should appear unless a body, guard, shoulder, elbow, or clearly staged physical impact has visibly caused it.

## 7. Risk / Failure Mode List

Key risks:

- character identity drift
- environment drift back to a generic courtyard or unrelated interior
- action unreadable because wall contact is too hidden or too fast
- wall impact not visible because characters block the impact center
- breakage too extreme or visually louder than the body action
- debris not causally connected to body/guard contact
- camera location/style jump away from the rainy temple side-wall reference
- accidental final/locked semantics
- architecture reference over-controlling empty-scene composition and preventing character/action integration
- character references over-controlling the scene and erasing the wall-panel target
- prompt overloading by mixing body impact, wall damage, rain, identity, and camera into one unclear priority stack

Mitigation:

- keep the wall target and impact center visible
- keep breakage localized and caused
- define reference duties before package creation
- keep one primary wall-impact action
- require human review before final/lock decisions
