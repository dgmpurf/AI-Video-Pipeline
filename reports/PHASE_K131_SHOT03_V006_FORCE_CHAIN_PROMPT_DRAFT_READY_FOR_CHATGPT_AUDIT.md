## V005 Failure Basis

SHOT-03 V005 is a useful terrain-affordance attempt, but it failed as a terrain-combat solution.

Recorded failure basis:

- Terrain-affordance attempt is present.
- Human review found soft force and low burst rhythm.
- Punch / leg speed did not match real force generation.
- The desired "哒哒哒" fast burst rhythm was missing.
- Terrain setup diluted contact density and power.
- K129 speed diagnostics were created, but speed-up cannot fix structural force-chain issues.
- K130 source-compliance audit found the missing core: no unbroken force chain from support foot / column foot point through knee compression, waist/hip torque, shoulder drive, and right forearm impact.

## V006 Design

V006 concept:

`short burst column-triggered force-chain impact`

Design principles:

- V006 is not another broad terrain-rebound prompt.
- Terrain contact is a short trigger, not an action display.
- The column base is only a `0.10s-0.18s` trigger.
- There is no jump display, no hang time, and no parkour display.
- Foot point contact must lead to impact within `0.12s-0.20s`.
- The complete force chain is written as one continuous kinetic sequence:
  support foot / column-foot compression -> knee -> waist/hip -> shoulder -> right forearm/hammerfist -> hit-stop -> guard compression -> follow-up.
- The clip keeps real speed, fast attack, fast guard, and fast counter.
- Dense follow-up contact beats are required after the main impact.

## Why No Structural Breakage

V006 isolates force-chain correction first.

V005 already showed that terrain-affordance can dilute contact density if the terrain action becomes the display subject. V006 therefore keeps the terrain intact and uses the column base only as a force trigger.

Controlled architectural impact should remain a later SHOT-04 direction after the force-chain issue is corrected:

- body impact into wooden lattice / side hall door / wall panel.
- rain / smoke / dust concealment.
- broken-entry suspense.
- object thrown into or out of a damaged opening.
