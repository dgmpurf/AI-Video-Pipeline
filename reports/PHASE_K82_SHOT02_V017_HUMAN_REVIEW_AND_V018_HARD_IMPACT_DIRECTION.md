## Diagnosis: Speed vs Prompt / Action Design

The weak impact is likely not purely a speed issue.

Slow speed contributes to softness, but the prompt/action design also contributes. V017 used many controlled-exchange action terms such as:

- wrist / forearm checks
- outside-angle redirect
- palm check
- elbow-line pressure
- shoulder bump
- forearm deflection

These terms help choreography readability, but they can bias the model toward demonstration-style sparring, push-hands rhythm, or polite controlled exchange rather than hard-impact contact.

V017 has only one explicitly strong contact beat. A local 1.15x / 1.25x speed diagnostic may improve perceived rhythm, but probably will not fully solve weak impact without better hard-contact reaction chains.

## Prompt Lesson

Future hard-impact prompts should include:

- Multiple contact beats early.
- Each contact beat has attack path, contact point, defender reaction, environment feedback, and rebound.
- Contact does not slide through the body.
- No long held-contact choreography.
- No demonstration-style pose transitions.
- No soft pushing exchange.
- No slow-motion-only impact.
