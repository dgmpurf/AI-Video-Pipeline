## 4. Primary Action-Composition Decision

K206 accepts the recommended R02 concept, with one important tightening:

The action must read as one explosive shoulder-check into crossed guard, causing a back/shoulder wall-panel impact and immediate rebound. It must not read as a sustained shove, wrestling hold, slow press, or static body pin.

Primary concept:

`Fenshou drives the action. Shuangji is redirected backward/sideways into the rainy wooden side-wall panel. Fenshou's lowered shoulder and braced forearm hit Shuangji's crossed guard; the force compresses her guard into her torso and drives her right shoulder / upper back into one visible wall panel between pillars. The wall reaction is localized at mid-height. Shuangji rebounds immediately into counter-ready posture.`

Required decisions:

- action driver: Fenshou
- compressed / redirected fighter: Shuangji
- contact type: Fenshou shoulder-check + forearm pressure into Shuangji crossed guard, causing Shuangji shoulder/upper-back-to-wall contact
- wall contact point: Shuangji right shoulder / upper back against the wall panel
- impact center: mid-height on one visible wooden wall panel between pillars
- force direction relative to camera: left-center to right-midground, slightly diagonal into the wall plane
- camera relation: side-corridor three-quarter view, wall plane visible on the right / rear side of frame
- starting distance: Shuangji begins within roughly half a step of the wall; Fenshou begins close enough that contact happens immediately, not after a long run-up
- cause-effect pose: Fenshou rear foot drives on wet stone, shoulder low, torso leaning forward; Shuangji knees bent, crossed guard compressed, rear/side shoulder hitting the wet wooden panel, one foot skidding on the floor
- one-frame read: Fenshou's forward force has slammed Shuangji's guard/body into the wall; the wall flex/splinter/rain response is caused by that exact contact point

## 5. Recommended Initial Concept Evaluation

Recommended concept from task:

> Shuangji is driven sideways/backward into the rainy wooden side-wall panel while Fenshou presses the attack from the corridor side. The impact center is mid-height on one visible wall panel between pillars. Shuangji's shoulder/back/guard is compressed against the wet wood; Fenshou's forward momentum reads as the cause. The wall response is localized: wet wood flex, small cracks, splinters, rain spray, and mist around the contact point. The corridor remains intact.

K206 decision:

Accepted with tightening.

Reason:

The concept directly addresses K190/K191 failure lessons by making architecture-as-combat visible and local. It uses the K202 wall-panel reference correctly and keeps the corridor intact. However, the phrase "presses the attack" could drift toward the old sustained pushing problem. Future prompt work should replace it with stronger result-first language:

`Fenshou lands one explosive shoulder-check into Shuangji's crossed guard, driving her shoulder and upper back into one wet wooden wall panel.`

Adjusted concept label:

`explosive shoulder-check into crossed guard -> Shuangji upper-back/shoulder wall impact -> localized wet-wood flex/crack/splinter response -> immediate rebound/counter-ready recovery`

## 6. Camera and Composition Plan

Camera:

- rainy side-corridor perspective
- three-quarter side view, not frontal courtyard
- camera stays at human fighting height
- no roof route, no overhead spectacle, no sudden interior hall drift
- no hard close-up that hides body-wall contact

Composition:

- wall plane remains visible on the right / rear side of the frame
- impact center sits on one readable wall panel between pillars
- pillar edges may frame the action but must not hide the contact point
- Fenshou occupies left-center / corridor-side foreground-midground
- Shuangji occupies right-center / wall-side midground
- wet stone floor remains visible under both fighters' feet
- rain and cold blue atmosphere remain visible
- bodies may overlap during impact, but wall contact must remain readable

Avoid:

- bodies fully covering the impact center
- wall panel turning into generic courtyard railing
- action drifting into a frontal main-hall courtyard
- characters floating away from the floor
- camera cutting to unrelated interior or roof space
- push/wrestling hold that lasts through the shot

## 8. Controlled Wall Response

Allowed wall reaction:

- localized panel flex at the exact shoulder/back contact center
- small wet cracks radiating from the contact point
- tiny wet wood splinters from the contact point
- rain spray / mist / dust at the contact center
- slight panel dent only around the impact
- water shake on the wall and floor near the impact

Not allowed:

- full wall collapse
- entire corridor destruction
- random holes
- unexplained debris before contact
- large splinter blast unrelated to force direction
- magic blast replacing body contact
- energy shockwave as the main event
- body embedded inside wall
- body-wall fusion
- gore

Damage timing rule:

No crack, splinter, dent, spray, or debris may appear before Shuangji's shoulder/upper back visibly contacts the wall panel. The wall response must be smaller than the body action and directly caused by it.
