## Recommended SPLIT-02 Primary Micro-Goal

Primary recommendation:

Column-edge guard compression + brief occlusion.

### Exact Physical Job

Two fighters remain in the exterior corridor. Fenshou pressures Shuangji's guard line toward a wooden column edge. Their forearms and shoulders compress briefly as the column partially occludes the body line. The pair passes the column edge in a tight, combat-driven movement and exits the occlusion still engaged.

### What Appears In Frame

- Exterior temple corridor with column, railing line, wet stone floor, rain, and stable V004-style visual language.
- Fenshou and Shuangji remain close, mid-shot readable, and identity-stable.
- One short column-edge compression beat.
- Brief half-body occlusion by the column.
- Immediate re-entry into close combat after the column edge.
- Small wet-floor skid or shoulder/guard recoil only if caused by the contact.

### What Must Not Appear

- No stepping on column base.
- No foot-on-random-stone.
- No jump.
- No hangtime.
- No parkour display.
- No roof, stairs, or eaves route.
- No full wall-run.
- No railing, door, lattice, column, or wall breakage.
- No structural cracks, debris, wood splinters, or spontaneous damage.
- No slow pose showcase.
- No static standoff.
- No final/lock decision.

### Why This Is Narrower Than V005/V006

V005/V006 attempted too much at once: terrain trigger, force-chain, high-speed combat density, and strong body consequence across a full 5s prompt. The K150 micro-goal reduces the job to one edit-friendly physical event: guard pressure into a column-edge occlusion. The terrain is an obstacle and staging tool, not the source of a full action system.

### How It Can Connect From CUT_B To CUT_C

- CUT_B can provide the incoming corridor-combat momentum.
- SPLIT-02 can insert a short column-edge compression/occlusion beat.
- CUT_C can function as return-to-close-combat after the terrain beat, especially if future SPLIT-02 ends with both fighters still engaged and moving laterally along the corridor.
