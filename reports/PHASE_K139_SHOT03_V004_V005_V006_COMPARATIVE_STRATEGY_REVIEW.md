## V004 Summary

### Strengths

- V004 is a positive clean corridor combat candidate.
- It improved rhythm, continuity, identity stability, and exterior corridor space over V003.
- It avoided the unwanted structural-damage problem.
- It is usable as a clean corridor combat baseline and fallback.
- It is also useful as a stable terrain/world reference because the exterior corridor, columns, railings, wet stone floor, and rainy temple space are readable.

### Weaknesses

- It remains too ordinary as corridor close combat.
- It does not sufficiently use temple terrain or architecture as a tactical fighting tool.
- It is not a complete SHOT-03 terrain-affordance solution.

### Candidate Value

V004 should be preserved as the SHOT-03 clean corridor baseline / fallback. It is not a final terrain-affordance solution, but it is the strongest stable reference among V004/V005/V006 for readable corridor combat.

## V005 Summary

### Strengths

- V005 visibly attempted terrain affordance.
- The terrain idea is present enough to teach what the model does when asked for a column-base / rebound-style action.
- It is a useful learning case.

### Weaknesses

- Human review found the fight too soft.
- Punch and leg attack speed did not match real force generation.
- Terrain setup diluted contact density and power.
- The foot plant / rebound did not read as a compact explosive force chain.
- The motion felt closer to soft jump, pose, or slow exchange than short explosive terrain-triggered impact.

### What It Taught

V005 showed that a terrain-affordance prompt can make the terrain idea visible, but visibility alone is not choreography quality. If the terrain moment receives too much timing weight, it turns into display or posing and drains force from the fight.

### Why Speed Diagnostic Was Insufficient

K129 speed diagnostics were useful as local tests, but K128/K130 already established that speed-up cannot repair structural force-chain failure. Source V1.11 supports this boundary: speed-up is useful only when the action chain, contact beats, identities, and spatial relationship already work.

## V006 Summary

### Strengths

- V006 was clause-level source-compliant.
- It explicitly encoded real speed, quick attack/guard/counter, short hit-stop, at least six contact beats, immediate re-entry, no full slow motion, no structural breakage, and a short column-base force trigger.
- It translated the V005 audit into a much stronger prompt.

### Weaknesses

- Human and ChatGPT review both found the result failed.
- The column-base / foot-trigger moment around the 1.35s-2.25s region did not read as credible architectural terrain.
- The terrain action looked like an odd stepping object / ground prop / pose-like move.
- Overall choreography still read like ordinary guard-punch exchange rather than a convincing martial force chain.
- Body consequences were too weak.
- Contact existed, but contact quality and choreography were not strong enough.

### What It Taught

V006 is the decisive evidence case: clause-level source compliance is not enough when the model cannot reliably translate a precise terrain-triggered martial force chain into credible motion.

### Why Prompt-Only Force-Chain Is Unreliable Here

This shot type asks for several difficult things at once: stable identity, stable corridor space, immediate close combat, a very short terrain trigger, complete body force transmission, dense contact beats, and believable opponent body consequence. V006 encoded those requirements, but the generated motion still failed at the terrain anchor and choreography level. Further pure prompt V007 attempts have low expected value unless the strategy changes.

## Route Options

### A. Return To Clean Corridor Combat

- Expected value: High for stable usable SHOT-03 material.
- Cost: Low. V004 already provides a positive baseline.
- Risk: Medium creative limitation; may underserve the user's terrain-affordance ambition.
- Required assets/references: V004 output, V004 prompt/report, existing corridor scene references.
- Suitable now: Yes.

This route preserves what works. It does not solve the terrain-affordance ambition, but it prevents SHOT-03 from stalling on unreliable terrain-triggered choreography.

### B. Split Terrain Assist Into Multiple Shots

- Expected value: High if the goal remains terrain-assisted combat.
- Cost: Medium. Requires shot decomposition, local edit planning, and likely multiple shorter generation tasks.
- Risk: Medium. Continuity and edit stitching need review, but each generated unit becomes simpler.
- Required assets/references: V004-style corridor baseline, selected still/keyframe anchors, possible local cut points, and explicit single-action micro-goals.
- Suitable now: Yes, but only as a strategy plan after human route approval.

This route converts "one complex 5s terrain force chain" into smaller units: setup / foot trigger / impact / reaction / continuation. It fits the evidence from V005 and V006 better than another single prompt-only 5-second attempt.

### C. Use Action Reference Video / Pose References

- Expected value: Medium to high for precise physical choreography.
- Cost: High. Requires finding or creating safe action references, pose frames, or staging references.
- Risk: Medium to high. Reference-duty clarity and identity protection become harder.
- Required assets/references: Action reference video or staged pose/keyframe set, plus identity references and scene references.
- Suitable now: Not as the immediate next phase unless the human wants a reference-building detour.

This is the right route if the project insists on precise column-base body mechanics, but it should be planned deliberately rather than folded into another pure prompt package.

### D. Move Architectural Interaction To SHOT-04

- Expected value: High for story escalation.
- Cost: Medium. Requires SHOT-04 planning and likely multiple shots.
- Risk: Medium. Architectural breakage needs strict causality and split-shot staging.
- Required assets/references: V004 corridor/world continuity, SHOT-04 architecture targets such as railing, wooden lattice, side-hall door, wall panel, smoke/rain/dust cover.
- Suitable now: Yes as a later escalation lane.

This route respects the story ambition while avoiding the current SHOT-03 failure mode. SHOT-04 can carry body impact into architecture, smoke/rain/dust concealment, broken opening suspense, and object interaction as separate shots.

## Recommended Route

Primary route: **B. Split terrain assist into multiple shorter edited shots, using V004-style clean corridor combat as the baseline.**

Do not create an immediate prompt-only V007. First, K140 should let the human decide a route and, if B is approved, define a split-shot micro-sequence where each shot has only one physical job:

1. establish continuing corridor combat from V004-like baseline;
2. isolate the terrain foot/column trigger as a very short setup or key pose;
3. generate the impact/reaction as its own clip;
4. bridge into the next action with an edit rather than forcing one 5-second generation to solve everything.

Fallback route: **A + D. Preserve V004 as the SHOT-03 clean corridor candidate and move heavier architecture interaction to SHOT-04.**

This fallback is the safest production route if the human prioritizes progress over more SHOT-03 terrain experimentation. It keeps SHOT-03 usable and gives architectural escalation a cleaner dedicated shot family.

Stop pure prompt-only V007 for now.
