## What Failed

V015 keeps too much of the action in a similar close hand-exchange pattern. The rhythm feels slow and regular rather than fast, sharp, and dangerous.

Main failure points:

- fight speed too slow
- repeated hand-to-hand and forearm exchange
- not enough attack variety
- insufficient impact and hit power
- micro-FX does not compensate for weak action design
- choreography does not create enough disadvantage, recovery, or tactical reversal

## Diagnosis

The weak impact is partly a speed issue, but the larger problem is action design.

The V015 direction over-emphasized similar wrist slap, forearm parry, palm check, block, and forearm bind motifs. Stretching that motif across the clip made the fight feel repetitive even when the character identity and visual baseline stayed acceptable.

The next attempt should not try to rescue V015 by simply speeding it up first. V016 should change the physical structure of the action.

## V016 Correction Direction

V016 should break the repetitive hand-exchange problem by adding lower-body fighting, line-stealing, off-balance pressure, and counter-re-entry.

Core progression:

1. Hand exchange starts but fails to break through.
2. Fenshou cuts his front foot inside to steal the line.
3. Shuangji steps diagonally outside.
4. A low kick or ankle sweep threat enters the exchange.
5. Shuangji lifts the knee or turns the shin to seal the line.
6. Upper-body parry continues while lower-body attacks happen.
7. One fighter is pushed off-line or forced into balance recovery.
8. Immediate counter-re-entry resumes pressure.
9. Final second continues fighting instead of posing out.

## V016 Force and Impact Principle

The next correction should show physical force through body mechanics rather than through large visual effects.

Required impact language:

- rear-foot push
- hip and waist rotation
- shoulder-driven compact strike
- short contact and rebound
- extremely short hit-stop only on key contacts
- low stance
- wet stone shoe skid
- small water splash
- robe hem flick
- sleeve snap
- hair whip
- visible balance recovery
- immediate re-entry
