## Long-Form Fast-Combat Direction

The human review identifies a promising direction: this fast combat can potentially be extended to 10s or even 15s.

Conditions for a longer fight:

- Camera work must vary while remaining readable.
- Action progression must keep adding layers.
- Attack rhythm should not simply repeat the same hand-blocking pattern.
- Intermediate beats should show escalation, disadvantage, recovery, and re-entry.

Multiple short fight clips may also be preferable to one long generated clip. A practical route is several 4s-5s clips that can be edited together into a 10s-15s fight passage.

The fight should progress through:

1. Fast hand exchange.
2. Footwork escalation.
3. Disadvantage or temporary loss of balance.
4. Counter-recovery and re-entry.

## Fast-Combat Power Principle

Fight speed can remain real-speed or slightly faster. Power should not depend only on slow motion.

Slow motion is allowed selectively, but it should not be the main source of impact.

Power should come from:

- rear-foot push
- hip/waist rotation
- shoulder-driven short strikes
- compact contact
- very short hit-stop
- arms rebounding from contact
- feet sliding on wet stone
- water splashes
- robe/sleeve snap
- hair whip
- visible recovery of balance and re-entry
- continuous pressure

The important requirement is power and impact at real speed: not soft fast motion and not only slow-motion heaviness.
