## 6. K183 Blueprint Review

### Premise

K183 uses one main architecture / terrain interaction: Fenshou pressures Shuangji into a corridor railing or wooden lattice, the railing/lattice responds only at the exact contact point, Shuangji rebounds immediately, and the shot ends mid-exchange.

This is structurally sound because it makes the architecture part of the fight rather than a decorative background.

### Composition Matrix

K183 defines a medium-wide 16:9 exterior side-corridor composition:

- Fenshou remains left-center, black-red male fighter identity, driving pressure from the wet stone floor.
- Shuangji remains right-center, blue-white / silver female fighter identity, compressed toward the railing/lattice.
- The railing/lattice occupies the right-side midground/background and only reacts at the shoulder-back / guard contact point.
- The wet stone floor supports visible skid, water splash, and foot pressure.
- Corridor posts, wall, roofline, rain, and temple depth remain stable world anchors.

This is a good use of the Composition Matrix layer because it locks screen zones, action anchors, reference responsibilities, and forbidden drift before any prompt/package creation.

### Natural-Language Translation

K183 translates the matrix into readable scene language: rainy exterior side corridor, medium-wide combat view, both fighters and foot contact points visible, railing/lattice as fixed structure, localized reaction after Shuangji contact, rebound/counter, and active cut.

This avoids relying on raw bbox coordinates as final model-facing language.

### Reference-Duty Plan

K183 separates reference duties correctly:

- Fenshou identity reference: identity only.
- Shuangji identity reference: identity only, highest-priority female-coded anchor.
- Corridor/scene reference: world and architecture only.
- Optional action/blocking reference: body pressure and guard compression only.
- Optional railing/lattice contact reference: localized architecture contact feedback only.

It also correctly prevents failed R01/R02 identity material from becoming identity references unless explicitly approved later.

### Timing and Contact-Beat Schedule

K183 includes a 0.00s-5.00s schedule:

- First clear contact by 0.00s-0.30s.
- Six contact/reaction/re-entry beats.
- Architecture contact beat at the railing/lattice.
- Rebound and counter.
- Final re-entry beat ending mid-exchange.

This satisfies the V1.11 early-contact, contact-density, and no-idle-tail direction.

- whole building damage

- Shuangji rebounds quickly and counters

- railing/lattice remains standing

- lattice
- railing
